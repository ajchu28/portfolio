The Core Curriculum

One major draw of The College at UChicago is The Core Curriculum. Introduced in 1930, The Core Curriculum has undergone six main phases, most recently reformed by Dean Boyer to reduce the class load and allow more flexibility for major courses. Despite its history and importance within the undergraduate education at the University of Chicago, UChicagoâ€™s website barely had mention of the Core, much less a comprehensive resource for learning more about the landscape and history of it.

From January through June of 2023, I worked with a team of faculty members and two other undergraduate researchers to overhaul The Core section of The College website. I was the primary web designer and developer, working with the team to organize all the information about The Core and distill it into an understandable format. Additionally, I redesigned the handout given to all incoming undergrads to help them gain a better understanding of the requirements and lay out of the Core. Check out the website here!
