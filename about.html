A fun fact about me is that at UChicago, I switched my major six times before finally landing at computer science and media arts and design. Going in to college, I used to say that I knew one thing about what I wanted to major in and one thing only: I didn't want to study computer science. However, the dabbling I did in Economics, Public Policy, Sociology, and Comparative Human Development before finally taking a computer science class gave me a lot of conviction that this is the path for me.

What the field of UX has taught me is that design is a universal language for communicating and a deep understanding of the technology underlying interaction only leads to more eloquent communication. This principal underlies the communication I strive for in my design.

Aside from UX, I also love food and cooking, playing ultimate frisbee, and seeing new places. I would love to connect!
