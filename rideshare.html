Visualizing Chicago Rideshare Cost

Chicago Open Data Portal is home to numerous data sets, chock full of interesting stories about Chicago and its inhabitants. Under an unassuming name—Transportation Network Providers—resides an itemized list of every single Uber or Lyft ride originating in or departing from Chicago. As someone who boasts an impressive number of trips on both services, I thought it would be interesting to dig deeper into this data set. Thus, my senior Media Arts and Design Thesis Project was born.

At the MAAD Expo in the Spring of 2023, I presented three views of ride cost in Chicago: an interactive map-based visualization, text-based contextualization of Hyde Park ride costs, and 3D prints of the cost of a ride per minute across the city at three times of day. With this project, I sought to explore the data set in three modes, learning about the interplay of mediums and the different facets of data these mediums uncover.

A takeaway? Uber and Lyft algorithms are masters of supply and demand. Another? The next thing is to make a pricing app to help users get the best deal based on historic prices with the ability to set parameters of pickup location and time.
